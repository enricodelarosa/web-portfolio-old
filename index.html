About Me

I have an AB Economics Degree. I worked as a Management Trainee for Coca Cola Philippines (under FEMSA) when I graduated from college. The FMCG industry is very fast paced and there are a lot of things that you are able to learn when you stay in one. Your social skills are also put to the test as accomplishing anything meaningful in the company requires you to go through corporate bureaucracy and several key personnel. Before finishing my stint in coca-cola as a management trainee, I had to help out in the family enterprise.

I started to sell rice. The business of selling rice can be very traditional. There was one day where I tried experimenting and opened our Lazada online store to sell... you guessed it. We sold rice online in the latter part of 2019 and no one ordered until a few months after. There were a couple weeks when we got a few orders. When the pandemic hit the online rice store blew up. At some point we were one of the top sellers in Lazada and we were one of the most reviewed stores that sold rice in the whole platform.

I used to hate math in highschool. I dreaded it because I was confused and clueless 90% of the time. My first math class in college changed all of that. I figured out that the key to math was just understanding it, which not a lot of teachers and professors can convey really clearly.

Why Am I in Web Development?

I am in Web Development because I think this is what I was made for. In my previous job, I had to create a payroll system that was based on google sheets. Basically the database used a google sheet and the logic part was handled by google appscript (written in javascript). I was a little bit familiar with javascript but I had no idea what objects and commands were used to access a google sheet object. I experimented and read a bit of documentation an after a couple of weeks and our payroll system was born. This happened several times in the company, new system meant that I had to create a new project/product for it. Not all my projects involved me creating a program, some of it were outside that realm and more management tasks.

I realized that the times when I was coding something was the time when I felt most alive. If I was working on a non-coding thing or a thing that didn't connect or relate to coding, I wouldn't want to go to work and get that job done. When I code, time just passes by. Sometimes I even forget to eat. Which is crazy because I love eating and in my previous work one of the things that I would think about during the morning was lunch and during the afternoon was dinner. When I code it does use a lot of energy, especially when the task is difficult or complicated and it requries long hours of focused work, but after a break I've always noticed that I would be ready to jump back in; as if the break was just a way to clear my mind and was a necessity to get the job done and not as a way to break free from the work. I guess the short answer to the question above is because this is what makes sense for me. They say that man's dignity is in his work. I say mine is in coding.
